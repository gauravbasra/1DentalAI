## Launch Post

Dental AI is being sold as a pile of point tools:

- an AI receptionist
- a clinical scribe
- an insurance checker
- a review tool
- another dashboard

That is not how dental practices work.

The real problem is the handoff:

- phone call to schedule
- schedule to insurance
- insurance to treatment estimate
- treatment plan to follow-up
- clinical note to claim support
- patient experience to review request

1DentalAI is built around those handoffs.

## Post 2: AI Receptionist

An AI receptionist does not create value just because it answers the phone.

The value is what happens after the call:

- Was the patient identified?
- Was the reason for calling understood?
- Did scheduling get the right context?
- Did insurance need to be checked?
- Did the treatment coordinator need a follow-up?
- Did the billing team need the message?

That is why 1DentalAI treats calls as workflow signals, not isolated transcripts.

AI receptionist page:

https://app.1dentalai.com/solutions/ai-receptionist-for-dentists

## Post 3: Insurance Verification

Dental insurance verification should not be a scavenger hunt.

Before tomorrow's schedule starts, the team should know:

- missing subscriber info
- eligibility status
- benefit evidence
- payer blockers
- prior auth risk
- financial clearance status
- who owns the next step

AI can help, but only if the workflow keeps evidence, review, and ownership clear.

https://app.1dentalai.com/solutions/dental-insurance-verification-ai

## Post 4: Clinical AI

Clinical AI scribing is not just about faster notes.

In dentistry, the safer question is:

What context does the provider need before approving the note?

That context may include:

- appointment reason
- forms
- medical history
- perio data
- treatment plan
- imaging context
- previous notes
- insurance or claim support needs

AI can draft. Providers approve.

https://app.1dentalai.com/solutions/clinical-ai-scribe-dentistry

## Community Post

Most dental AI conversations start with the wrong question:

"Which tool should I buy?"

Better question:

"Which workflow is leaking the most time?"

Start with these seven:

1. Missed calls to booked appointments
2. Insurance verification before tomorrow's schedule
3. Claims needing attachments, narratives, or payer follow-up
4. Treatment plans presented but not scheduled
5. Clinical notes waiting for provider review
6. Review requests that should be delayed because the patient had a poor experience
7. Location analytics that do not show who owns the next step
